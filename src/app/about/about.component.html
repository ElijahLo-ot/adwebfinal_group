Other issues of gender inequality of different countries around the globe:

1. The gender pay gap

- Some statistics shows that women are paid less than 80 percent than men. At some other cases, women are also discriminated with racism and resulted of uneven or poor salaries payment for their work.


2. Gender disparities in agriculture

- Women have less access to important tools and resources such as fertilizer, seeds, training, and farm labor. Even when they have equal access to these resources, it does not always result in income equality. Women, as primary caregivers for children, face challenges in getting their goods to market, particularly in rural areas. Reduced harvests and access result in lower income, which is especially harmful so even though women tend to reinvest their income back into their families and communities. When women do not earn as much as men, everyone suffers.


3. Lack of employment equality

- The division of labor is one of the causes of gender inequality in the workplace. Most societies have an ingrained belief that men are simply better suited to certain jobs. Most of the time, those are the highest-paying jobs. Women's income is reduced as a result of this discrimination. Women also bear the primary responsibility for unpaid labor, so even when they work full-time, they have extra work that is never compensated financially.
